* The standard playbook for developing high performing teams is wrong - its laser focus on productivity and efficiency can actually impede performance when things get ambiguous and complex
* Yet as problems become more urgent we return to the same dysfunctional playbook, doubling down on business as usual
* Teams that perform well in complex contexts already know this - they obsess less over productivity/efficiency and more on team health
* A healthy team grows and thrives when the conditions are right
* A team can't be engineered to perfection because it's made of people not parts 
	* People develop throughout their whole lives, never reaching a finished form
	* The garden as a developing system provides a better way to think of a team
* Five fundamental conditions for g rowing a healthy team
	* 1. Safety
	* 2. Learning
	* 3. Listening
	* 4. Purpose
	* 5. Autonomy

1. Safety is not fluff and there's a lot of research showing how hard it is for people to be creative when they're afraid. Team members can only do the hard work of being creative contributors and problem solvers when they feel safe.
